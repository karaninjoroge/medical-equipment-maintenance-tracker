## The Problem in One Sentence

Public health facilities in Kenya regularly experience equipment downtime because maintenance is reactive, undocumented, and uncoordinated — leading to preventable failures in patient care.

---

## Project Summary

This case study documents the product management process for a B2B SaaS maintenance tracker designed for medical equipment in public health facilities. Unlike the other projects in this portfolio, this is a pure B2B product where the buyer (facility administrator or county health department) and the user (biomedical technician) are different people.

---

## Why My Background Matters Here

As a Biomedical Engineering student, I have studied the lifecycle of medical equipment — calibration, preventive maintenance schedules, regulatory requirements, and failure modes. This domain knowledge shapes the product requirements in ways a non-technical PM would miss.
